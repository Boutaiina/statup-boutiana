About the Disease

The patient has been suffering from diabetes for four years

Type 2 diabetes is a condition where the body either becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough. It is the most common form of diabetes, often developing in adults, but can also occur in children due to lifestyle factors. Common risk factors include obesity, physical inactivity, and family history. Symptoms may include increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, and blurred vision. Type 2 diabetes can lead to serious complications like heart disease, kidney damage, and nerve issues if not managed properly. Treatment involves lifestyle changes, including a balanced diet, regular exercise, and sometimes medication or insulin. Early diagnosis and management are crucial to prevent long-term health issues. Regular blood sugar monitoring is key to controlling the condition.
